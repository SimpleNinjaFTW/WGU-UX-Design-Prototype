Welcome to Taniti

Come Visit Taniti!

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano

Transportation

Getting around the island

Arrival

Getting to the island

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.

Getting Around

Transportation options

  • Rental cars
  • Taxis
  • Buses
  • Bikes
  • Walkable areas
Rental Cars

Flexible travel

Travel on your own time. Rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law)

Walking

Explore on foot

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.

Transit

Public transportation

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. Additionally, Taxis are available in Taniti City

Travel Information

Important Details

Money

Currency and payments

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.

Language

Communication

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

Safety

Health and safety

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Please ensure that you safeguard your belongings

Planning

Rules

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18.

Dining

Food

Taniti has many restaurants, including fish & rice, American-Style meals, and Pan Asian cuisine. Additionally, Taniti has supermarkets and various other grocery options.

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so plan accordingly!
